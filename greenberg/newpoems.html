Greenberg's life as a writer lasted only a handful of years (from 1912 to early 1917), but during those years, he wrote over six hundred poems and other works -- both in notebooks (fifteen or more) and on loose pages (everything from fine stationary and graph paper to toilet tissue and blank hospital exam forms). The selection here consists mostly of works he wrote during 1915 and 1916, arranged in the same sets and same order as they appear in his manuscripts. To view a set online, select its title.
